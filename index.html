About Me

I am a wildlife ecologist and snow scientist. Originally from the DC-metro area, I became in awe of snow when I hiked the John Muir Trail in 2017, a high snow year. I later went on to hike the Pacific Crest Trail in 2019, also in a high snow year. I speak norwegian. A Seattelite of three years, I enjoy running, biking, and skiing. I love bringing friends into the outdoors and certified Wilderness First Responder and AIARE Level 1 certification. An advocate that the outdoors are both city parks and national parks, I love exploring the outdoors of Seattle life including restaurants, museums, and local book stores.

Research

I am a wildlife ecologist studying the effects of changing on wildlife. Warming winters are changing the way that animals move, eat, hunt, and sleep. Using wildlife cameras, we can detect both where animals are occuring and the amount of snow. Connecting the two together allows us to explore questions like, "How is snow changing where deer move? When they move? How might this affect interactions with other animals in the landscape, like wolves and lynx?"

I use machine learning and remote sensing.
